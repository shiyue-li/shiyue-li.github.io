Curriculum Vitae

Curiouser and curiouser!

I’m a second year PhD student, and a Lang Fellow at Yale Math. My advisor is Melody Chan.

My current research interest is to study enumerative and intersection problems in algebraic geometry and arithmetic geometry, using techniques from combinatorics and representation theory.

In Spring 2019, I’m a visiting student at Brown and co-organizing the Brown DRP with Kyle Ferendo and Jiahua Zou.

In Fall 2018, I’m the organizer of Yale DRP.

In Summer 2018, I’m a mentor at Canada/USA Mathcamp 2018.

I converged to my current stage of life via wonderful four years at Harvey Mudd College studying math, CS and music.

At Harvey Mudd, I worked with Dagan Karp on thesis Tropical Derivation of Cohomology Ring of Heavy/light Hassett Spaces – a fun project proposed by Dhruv Ranganathan

Before becoming a math major in senior year, I was an absent-minded programmer and was a software engineer intern at Google Research, advised by Caroline Pantofaru and Malcolm Slaney.
